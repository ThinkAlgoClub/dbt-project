## 6. The Stakeholder Question – "How many students are currently enrolled across all our Texas campuses?"

### Why This Question Is a Trap

The regional superintendent asks: *"How many students are currently enrolled across all our Texas campuses?"*

An absolute beginner would write `SELECT COUNT(*) FROM dim_students WHERE state = 'TX' AND is_currently_enrolled = TRUE` and call it a day.

**That answer would be wrong.** Why? Because the question has **at least six hidden ambiguities**.

### The Six Ambiguities (What You Must Clarify)

Before writing a single line of SQL, you need to ask clarifying questions. Let's break down each ambiguous phrase.

| Ambiguous Phrase | The Hidden Question | Why It Matters |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"currently enrolled"** | Does "currently" mean today's date? Or the current academic term (Fall 2025)? Or the most recent snapshot (last Friday)? | A student who withdrew yesterday is not enrolled today. A student who starts next week is not enrolled today. |
| **"students"** | Does this count unique students (one row per child) or total enrollments (students can be in multiple schools)? | In your data, a student cannot be at two Texas schools simultaneously (by business rule), but what about a student who transferred from Ohio to Texas? Do they count for Texas? |
| **"across all Texas campuses"** | Does this include campuses that are currently closed for summer? Does it include online-only campuses? What about campuses that opened last week with zero students? | If a campus has no students, should it be excluded or included as zero? |
| **"enrolled"** | What is the definition of enrolled? Does a student on a long-term suspension count? Does a student who has not attended for 10 days still count? | State funding definitions may differ from operational definitions. |
| **"current" point in time** | What timestamp are we using? Data is never real-time. If a student enrolled this morning but the data hasn't synced, do we exclude them? | You need a data latency agreement (e.g., "as of end of previous day"). |
| **"Texas" boundary** | Does a student attending a Texas campus but living in Oklahoma count? (Yes – location of campus, not residence.) Does a Texas campus include pre-K programs? | Some districts exclude pre-K from "enrollment" counts for certain reports. |

### Your Response Framework (Walk Them Through)

In the interview, you will **not** give an answer. You will walk the panel through your thinking. Here's a script:

> *"Before I write any SQL, I need to ask the superintendent clarifying questions. Here's what I would ask:*

> *1. **Time definition:** 'Currently' – do you mean as of today's date, or as of the most recent completed school day? And what time of day? Our data warehouse updates nightly at 2 AM, so there's a 24-hour lag. Is that acceptable?*

> *2. **Enrollment definition:** What constitutes 'enrolled'? Does a student with a withdrawal date in the future but who has stopped attending count? Does a student who is on a long-term suspension count? I'll need to align with state funding definitions or our internal attendance policy.*

> *3. **Campus scope:** Does 'Texas campuses' include any IDEA campus physically in Texas, regardless of grade levels? What about a campus that is temporarily closed for renovation?*

> *4. **Student uniqueness:** Are we counting unique students? If a student transferred from an Ohio campus to a Texas campus mid-year, would you want them included? (I assume yes, because they are now attending a Texas campus.)*

> *5. **Exclusions:** Should we exclude students who have graduated early, or those in pre-K if your question is about K-12 only?*

> *6. **Data freshness:** If the official 'current' snapshot is always as of midnight last night, I'll document that. Would you like a real-time estimate or a certified daily number?*

> *After getting answers, I would write the SQL. For example, if they say 'unique students as of today at Texas campuses, excluding pre-K, using nightly snapshot', then the SQL is clear.*

> *But the most important step is **documenting the definition** so every report going forward uses the same logic."*

### How to Document the Agreed Definition (So It's Consistent)

You cannot rely on memory or Slack messages. You need a **living documentation artifact**. In dbt, you can add descriptions to models and columns. For this specific metric, you would create a **semantic layer** or a **metric definition**.

**Example documentation in dbt** (in your `schema.yml` or a markdown file):

```yaml
metrics:
  - name: currently_enrolled_students_texas
    description: |
      Official definition as of 2025-01-15, approved by Regional Superintendent.
      Count of unique students who have an active enrollment at any Texas-based IDEA campus
      as of the most recent daily snapshot (end of prior day, 11:59 PM local time).
    calculation: |
      SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT student_id)
      FROM {{ ref('fct_enrollments') }}
      WHERE state_code = 'TX'
        AND is_open_enrollment = TRUE
        AND enrollment_start_date <= CURRENT_DATE()
        AND (enrollment_end_date IS NULL OR enrollment_end_date >= CURRENT_DATE())
        AND grade_level_int BETWEEN 1 AND 12  -- Excludes Pre-K (0) and missing
    caveats: |
      - Does not include students who enrolled today but are not yet in Snowflake (24h latency).
      - Does not include withdrawn or transferred students, even if exit_date is future-dated.
      - Students attending Texas IDEA campuses but living out of state are included.
    refresh_frequency: Daily at 6 AM.
    owner: Analytics Engineering Team
```

### What If They Push Back and Say "Just Give Me a Number"?

If the superintendent insists on an immediate answer without clarifying, you do **not** guess. Instead:

> *"I can give you a provisional number based on our current data definitions, but I strongly recommend we align on the definition first. I'll provide the number with a clear disclaimer: 'As of last night's snapshot, using the definition of active enrollment without exit date, excluding pre-K, the count is X.' Then I'll schedule a 15-minute meeting to lock in the official definition for future reports."*

This shows **professional judgment** – you prioritize accuracy over speed.

### Applying to Your Interview (What You Will Say in Part 3)

You have 5 minutes. Structure your answer:

1. **Restate the question** (30 seconds): "The superintendent asks: How many students are currently enrolled across all Texas campuses?"

2. **List 3-4 key clarifications** (2 minutes): Focus on time definition, enrollment definition (active vs. withdrawn), and whether pre-K is included. Also ask about data latency tolerance.

3. **Propose a documentation method** (1 minute): "I would add a metric definition in dbt's YAML with description, caveats, and owner. I would also create a 'Definition of Enrolled Students' page in our data catalog (e.g., Atlan or a Confluence wiki)."

4. **Show an example SQL that implements the agreed definition** (1 minute): Write a simple query that uses `fct_enrollments` with `is_open_enrollment = TRUE` and `state_code = 'TX'`, plus date filters.

5. **Highlight remaining ambiguities** (30 seconds): "Even after defining, there will be edge cases – a student who withdrew today but data hasn't updated. We document these caveats so users know the number is as of last night."
